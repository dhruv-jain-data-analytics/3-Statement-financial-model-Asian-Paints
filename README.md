Asian Paints Financial Statement Analysis & 3-Statement Model

Project Overview
This project presents a comprehensive financial analysis of Asian Paints using a 3-Statement Financial Model. The analysis covers the Income Statement, Balance Sheet, Cash Flow implications, and key financial ratios to evaluate the company's financial health, profitability, operational efficiency, and long-term growth prospects.

Business Objective
Analyze the financial performance of Asian Paints over multiple years.
Evaluate profitability, growth, and shareholder returns.
Assess working capital management efficiency.
Understand capital allocation and leverage strategy.
Generate actionable investment and business insights.

Income Statement Insights
Revenue demonstrates a strong long-term growth trend despite temporary volatility in FY22 and FY25.
EBITDA has expanded consistently over the years, indicating strong operational performance.
Several income statement items declined in FY25, suggesting short-term margin pressure.
EPS has increased consistently, reflecting sustainable earnings growth.
Dividend per Share has also increased steadily, indicating strong cash generation and shareholder focus.

Balance Sheet Insights
Investments increased significantly around FY21, reflecting strategic capital allocation.
Inventory levels have risen over time, indicating increasing working capital requirements.
The company maintains very low leverage, demonstrating strong financial stability and a conservative capital structure.

Ratio Analysis Insights
Profitability
ROE remains strong, indicating attractive shareholder returns.
ROCE has gradually declined, suggesting capital employed is growing faster than operating profits.
Liquidity & Efficiency
Debtor Days have increased over time, indicating slower receivable collections.
Inventory Days have also increased, resulting in more capital being tied up in inventory.
Cash Conversion Cycle has expanded due to rising working capital intensity.

Solvency
Interest Coverage Ratio remains extremely strong, indicating minimal debt servicing risk.

Key Findings
Strong long-term revenue and earnings growth.
Stable dividend policy and consistent shareholder value creation.
Conservative balance sheet with very low leverage.
Increasing working capital requirements are putting pressure on operational efficiency.
Declining ROCE highlights the need for improved returns on invested capital.

Recommendations
Improve receivables collection efficiency to reduce debtor days.
Strengthen inventory management practices to optimize working capital.
Focus on generating higher returns from recent investments.
Improve operational efficiency to stabilize ROCE.
Protect margins against raw material price volatility through cost management initiatives.

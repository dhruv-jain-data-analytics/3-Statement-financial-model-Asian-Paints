# Asian Paints Financial Statement Analysis & 3-Statement Model

## Project Overview

This project analyzes the financial performance of Asian Paints using a 3-Statement Financial Model. The analysis evaluates the company's profitability, growth, financial stability, capital efficiency, and working capital management.

## Tools Used

- Microsoft Excel
- Financial Modeling
- Ratio Analysis
- Financial Statement Analysis
- Data Visualization

## Skills Demonstrated

- 3-Statement Financial Modeling
- Financial Analysis
- Ratio Analysis
- Working Capital Analysis
- Corporate Finance
- Investment Research

## Executive Summary

Asian Paints demonstrates strong long-term growth, healthy profitability, and a stable financial position. However, increasing working capital intensity is putting pressure on capital efficiency metrics.

---

## Income Statement Insights

- Revenue shows a strong long-term growth trend.
- Revenue volatility was observed in FY22 and FY25 due to demand cycles and raw material price fluctuations.
- EBITDA has expanded consistently, indicating strong operational performance.
- EPS has increased steadily over the years.
- Dividend Per Share has followed a consistent upward trend, reflecting strong shareholder returns.
- FY25 witnessed pressure on several income statement items, suggesting short-term margin challenges.

---

## Balance Sheet Insights

- Investments increased significantly around FY21, indicating strategic capital allocation.
- Inventory levels have risen steadily over time.
- Working capital requirements have increased.
- The company maintains very low leverage and a conservative capital structure.
- Financial stability remains strong.

---

## Ratio Analysis Insights

### Profitability

- ROE remains strong and continues to generate attractive shareholder returns.
- ROCE has gradually declined, indicating capital employed is growing faster than operating profits.

### Liquidity & Efficiency

- Debtor Days have increased, indicating slower receivable collections.
- Inventory Days have increased, resulting in more capital being tied up in inventory.
- Cash Conversion Cycle has expanded due to higher working capital intensity.

### Solvency

- Interest Coverage Ratio remains exceptionally strong.
- Debt-related financial risk remains minimal.

---

## Key Findings

✅ Strong revenue growth over the long term

✅ Consistent earnings and dividend growth

✅ Low debt and strong financial stability

✅ Increasing working capital requirements

✅ Declining ROCE requires management attention

---

## Recommendations

1. Improve receivable collection efficiency.
2. Strengthen working capital management.
3. Optimize inventory levels.
4. Focus on generating higher returns from new investments.
5. Improve cost efficiency to protect margins during raw material price volatility.
